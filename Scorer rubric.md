# Quality Assessment Rubric for Clinical Trial Eligibility Criteria

## I. Framework Overview

This rubric evaluates eligibility criteria across **three** dimensions. Each dimension is scored on a scale of 1–10.

| Dimension | Core Objective |
| --- | --- |
| **Safety** | Protecting participant welfare through drug-specific risk screens, contraindication coverage, and physiological safety boundaries. |
| **Efficacy** | Aligning inclusion/exclusion decisions with the intervention's mechanism of action so the primary endpoint can be measured with adequate signal-to-noise. |
| **Recruitment** | Ensuring a sufficient, accessible, and operationally enrollable patient pool exists in real-world settings. |

---

## II. Detailed Scoring Criteria (1–10 Scale)

### 1. Safety

*Assessment: Coverage of drug-specific contraindications, organ-function safeguards, drug–drug interaction (DDI) exclusions, and population-specific risk containment.*

**Sub-aspects evaluated:**
- **Risk mitigation** — organ function thresholds (hepatic/renal), cardiovascular exclusion specificity, known class-effect risks (e.g., QTc prolongation, bleeding).
- **Contraindication coverage** — hypersensitivity, drug–drug interactions, condition-specific exclusions that match the intervention's pharmacological profile.
- **Population-specific risk containment** — pregnancy/lactation exclusion where appropriate, vulnerable subgroup protection, dose-toxicity boundary criteria.

* **9–10 (Exceptional)**: Comprehensive, drug-specific exclusions with clear physiological thresholds; covers DDIs, organ dysfunction, and population-specific risks relevant to the mechanism.
* **7–8 (Robust)**: Aligns with GCP standards; clear safety boundaries for major risk categories; minor gaps in DDI or borderline population coverage.
* **5–6 (Moderate)**: Safety boundaries are vague or generic; potentially includes borderline patients who may be hypersensitive to drug toxicity; missing one or two relevant contraindication categories.
* **3–4 (Marginal)**: Fails to account for known Black Box warnings or specific class-effect risks; safety exclusions are either absent or overly broad without clinical justification.
* **1–2 (Critical)**: Significant ethical or safety flaws; poses unacceptable risk of SAEs; invites regulatory intervention.

### 2. Efficacy

*Assessment: Alignment between the drug's mechanism of action and the participant's biological profile, disease state, and endpoint measurability.*

**Sub-aspects evaluated:**
- **Mechanism alignment** — inclusion criteria select patients whose biological profile (biomarker status, disease stage, severity) matches the intervention's pharmacological target.
- **Outcome measurability** — endpoint-relevant confounders are excluded; baseline measurability requirements are specified; competing risks that would mask the treatment effect are controlled.
- **Signal-to-noise optimization** — criteria reduce heterogeneity without unnecessary narrowing; patients unlikely to respond (biologically resistant, irreversible disease state) are appropriately excluded.

* **9–10 (Exceptional)**: Precise targeting of mechanism-relevant phenotypes in a high-signal window; confounders controlled; endpoint measurable with adequate power.
* **7–8 (Robust)**: Strong enrichment of clinical phenotypes; expected event rates sufficient; minor gaps in confounder control.
* **5–6 (Moderate)**: Moderate heterogeneity; includes patients with low baseline severity or stable disease, potentially diluting the treatment effect; some confounders uncontrolled.
* **3–4 (Marginal)**: Overly broad criteria or poor mechanistic alignment; high risk of null result due to insufficient signal-to-noise ratio.
* **1–2 (Critical)**: Cohort is biologically resistant to the intervention or the disease state is irreversible, precluding any observable benefit.

### 3. Recruitment

*Assessment: The intersection of criteria breadth, real-world epidemiological prevalence, and operational enrollment feasibility.*

**Sub-aspects evaluated:**
- **Population reach** — criteria stringency vs. real-world patient volume; no arbitrary or unjustified exclusions that shrink the eligible pool.
- **Enrollment feasibility** — screening procedures rely on standard-of-care diagnostics; screen failure rates are predictable; site burden is manageable.
- **Cohort accessibility** — criteria align with standard patient flow at typical clinical centers; geographic, logistical, or temporal barriers are minimized.

* **9–10 (Exceptional)**: Aligns with the majority of the real-world patient population; no arbitrary exclusions; facilitates rapid enrollment with standard screening.
* **7–8 (Robust)**: Specific restrictions exist but align with standard patient flow at major clinical centers; manageable screen failure rate.
* **5–6 (Moderate)**: Multiple restrictive layers (e.g., specific prior therapies + narrow age range + no comorbidities) significantly shrink the eligible pool.
* **3–4 (Challenging)**: Seeking "Unicorn" patients; criteria detached from clinical reality; stagnant accrual expected.
* **1–2 (Critical)**: Theoretical population only; high risk of trial termination due to enrollment failure.
